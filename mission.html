Forged in Appalachia

Coming from a small Appalachian village of just 1,300 people, the lack of traditional opportunities wasn't a barrier—it was a catalyst. I realized early on that I didn't want to be a starving artist, so I held regular jobs—even working at a Victoria's Secret warehouse in Columbus—while fiercely pursuing my creative and technical passions.

I've been deeply involved in multimedia content creation, web development, and digital operations since high school, hacking together encoding layouts during the golden era of MySpace. Over the years, that technical foundation allowed me to cross industries fluidly, leading to unique opportunities like writing music for a subsidiary label of Universal Republic.

Local Impact, Global Scale

My ultimate goal has always been to stay in my community and make it better, documenting its rich history—like the UNESCO World Heritage geometric earthworks left by the Indigenous Hopewell people.

The Science of Information

The core of PINK PILLED™ relies on managing complex datasets, understanding information transfer, and executing rigorous scientific protocols. This isn't just theory; it's active practice.

I am currently studying Health and Computer Sciences at the University of the People. Maintaining a 4.0 GPA at a tuition free accredited institution. I combined this technical rigor with my creative background to launch the girl ben Elohim experiment. A performance art project continuing Richard Dawkins' 1970s memetics experiments, testing how information transmits and relates to math, Boolean algebra, and logic.

Today, I apply these exact methodologies as a Project Manager for an independent mathematician working on a unified physics field theory, handling their institutional stewardship and complex scientific communications.

Ground Truth Operations

You cannot engineer solutions for the future if you are disconnected from the reality of the present.

  • Community Infrastructure

    Working as a pastoral assistant at a local Methodist church, I rebuild digital infrastructure (websites, live streams) while managing critical administrative operations.

  • Advocacy & Support

    I actively coordinate on-the-ground services for the unhoused community, organizing daily showers, meals, laundry, and clothing distribution. Technology means nothing without humanity.

  • Federal Empowerment

    I registered a formal business to pursue federal contracting through SAM.gov specifically to create pathways for traditionally disadvantaged groups. I want to help other women see themselves differently, utilize their unique talents, and take up space in ecosystems that previously excluded them.
