Our Genesis

The drive to protect innovators and secure intellectual capital runs deep in our DNA. Our founder's family history carries a stark lesson: her great-grandmother's third late husband, Leonard Stoneburner, invented inkjet printing. Despite his brilliance, he was systematically exploited by his employer, coerced into surrendering his intellectual property, and watched from the sidelines as his revolutionary technology was ultimately sold to Kodak.

His story is a visceral reminder of how easily technological surprise can happen. When we look at the core mission of defense research agencies like DARPA—preventing technological surprise and maintaining operational superiority—it becomes clear that true national security requires rigorous institutional stewardship. Technological surprise doesn't just happen because an adversary out-innovates us; it happens when our own systems exploit, gatekeep, and alienate the very creators capable of building the future.

Founded in 2025, PINK PILLED™ was born out of this generational realization. We observed that the federal acquisition lifecycle is fundamentally flawed, often pushing away the brightest minds due to technical debt, bureaucratic red tape, and predatory IP practices.

We realized that to truly accelerate defense acquisition and R&D, we couldn't just play by the old rules. We built PINK PILLED™ to act as the institutional steward Leonard never had—a bridge between commercial innovation, historically disadvantaged populations, and mission-critical government requirements. By actively scouting talent from marginalized groups and relentlessly protecting their IP, we level the playing field and ensure the next generation of revolutionary tech serves the nation that needs it.
